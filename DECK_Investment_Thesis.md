## The Short Version

Deckers is a two-brand story — HOKA growing fast, UGG growing steadily, and everything else being phased out. The business is excellent: no debt, $1.9B in cash, and over $1B in annual free cash flow. The stock is not.

At ~$109, the market is pricing in continued execution with no margin of safety. My DCF at a 10% WACC and 3% terminal growth rate gives me the same number — roughly $109. That's not a buy signal. That's fairly valued with no room for error.

The Street's average target is around $127, which implies margin recovery that management itself isn't guiding to. I'd rather be the conservative voice and wait for a better entry point around $88–$95 — roughly a 15–20% discount to fair value.

---

## What Deckers Actually Is

Deckers sells footwear. Three brands matter:

**HOKA** is the growth engine. Performance running shoes that have gone from a niche ultra-running brand to a mainstream category. Revenue has grown from $1.4B in FY2023 to $2.6B in FY2026 — nearly doubling in three years. The deceleration story is the whole thesis: FY2023 was +90% growth, FY2024 was +28%, FY2025 was +23%, FY2026 came in at +15.9%. Management guided low-double-digit growth for FY2027. The trajectory is clear. The question is whether it stabilizes here or keeps sliding.

**UGG** is the cash engine. Seasonal, mature, and more resilient than people expect. FY2026 revenue was $2.74B, growing ~13% — better than anyone expected for a brand that was supposed to be fading. UGG funds the HOKA investment cycle and makes the balance sheet look as good as it does.

**Teva and other brands** are being wound down. $221M in FY2025, declining. Ignore them.

Fiscal year ends March 31. FY2026 results were reported in May 2026.

## The Numbers

**FY2026 actuals:**
Revenue $5.47B | Gross margin 57.7% | Operating income $1.26B | EPS $7.02 | Cash $1.91B | Debt $0

**FY2027 management guidance:**
Revenue $5.86–5.91B | Gross margin ~56.5% | SG&A ~35% of revenue | Operating margin ~21.5% | EPS $7.30–7.45

The margin compression between FY2026 and FY2027 is the most important thing in this thesis. Gross margin is guided down ~120bps, SG&A is guided higher, and operating margin drops from 23.1% to ~21.5%. This is not a disaster — it's a deliberate investment cycle. They are spending on brand, international expansion, and DTC infrastructure. The bet is that it pays off in FY2028 and beyond. My model gives that bet a reasonable chance, but at current prices you're not being compensated for the risk if it doesn't.

## What My Model Says

I built a 3-statement model off the FY2023–FY2025 10-Ks with projections through FY2028. Every historical line was verified against the SEC filings. My FY2026 estimate came within 0.3% of the actual reported revenue, which gave me confidence the model was well-calibrated.

For FY2027, I used management guidance on margins: 56.5% gross, 35% SG&A, ~21.5% EBIT. My revenue assumption of ~$5.98B is slightly above the top of their guidance range of $5.86–5.91B, so I'm mildly optimistic on the top line while being disciplined on margins.

The DCF uses a 10% WACC — built from CAPM: 4.5% risk-free rate, 1.15 beta, 5% equity risk premium. Deckers has no debt, so WACC equals cost of equity. At 3% terminal growth, I get an enterprise value of ~$13.5B. Adding back $1.9B net cash and dividing by 141.5M diluted shares gives me ~$109 implied price.

One honest limitation: the terminal value carries about 80% of the enterprise value. That's the nature of a 3-year explicit forecast period — you're mostly betting on the terminal growth assumption. I've stress-tested it in the sensitivity table, but it's worth acknowledging.

## Bull Case (~$129)

HOKA reaccelerates internationally. Europe and Asia are genuinely underpenetrated — the brand's US success hasn't fully translated globally, and if it does, the revenue ceiling is much higher than current estimates. Pair that with gross margins recovering faster than guided as tariff headwinds fade and DTC mix improves, and you get meaningful operating leverage.

At 9% WACC and 3.5% terminal growth, I get to ~$129.

What would make me more bullish: two consecutive quarters of HOKA growth above expectations, any upward revision to gross margin guidance, and international revenue mix breaking above 35% of total.

---

## Bear Case (~$88)

HOKA growth decelerates to mid-single digits faster than the market expects. On Running, Brooks, and New Balance are not sleeping. Performance running is competitive and brand heat can fade quickly. If HOKA growth lands at 5–7% and gross margins stay compressed because they're forced to promote volume, operating deleverage accelerates. The stock re-rates from ~15x earnings to ~12x on a narrative shift from growth to mature brand.

At 11% WACC and 2.5% terminal growth, I get to ~$88.

What would make me more bearish: HOKA North America volumes flattening, any gross margin guidance cut, or management commentary suggesting pricing power is eroding.

## Why Capital Allocation Partially Offsets the Margin Story

Deckers repurchased $1.075B of stock in FY2026 — nearly double the $567M they bought back in FY2025. The board then increased the buyback authorization by $3.5B in May 2026, bringing total remaining authorization to roughly $5B. With $1.9B in cash and ~$1B of annual FCF, they have real capacity to keep reducing share count. This structurally supports EPS growth even through a margin-compression year, and it's worth giving them credit for it.

## What to Watch

The next data point is Q1 FY2027 earnings, expected in July 2026. Three numbers matter:

1. **HOKA revenue growth** — above 15% is bullish; below 10% suggests the deceleration is sharper than guided
2. **Gross margin vs. guidance** — 56.5% is the bar; a beat here is the single biggest upside catalyst
3. **International revenue mix** — rising mix means HOKA has runway; flat or declining signals North America saturation
